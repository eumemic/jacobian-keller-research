# JC2 frontier claims: source audit

**Scope.** Plane complex Keller maps \(F=(P,Q):\mathbb C^2\to\mathbb C^2\), \([P,Q]\in\mathbb C^*\), unless stated otherwise. Search cutoff for item 7: **2026-07-20 12:40 America/Los_Angeles (19:40 UTC)**. “Geometric degree” means \([\mathbb C(x,y):\mathbb C(P,Q)]\), not total polynomial degree.

| # | Verdict | Audit |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Overstated; correct with `<125`, not `≤125`.** | Guccione–Guccione–Horruitiner–Valqui classify hypothetical pairs with \(\max\{\deg P,\deg Q\}<125\). Their **Theorem 2.1 (p. 2)** says a counterexample has either maximum degree \(\ge125\), or pair \((72,108)\)/(\(108,72\)). Thus \((72,108)\) is the sole unordered pair left **below 125**, and the first unruled-out pair in that ordering, but their theorem does not classify the boundary \(\max=125\). [arXiv:2204.14178v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.14178), submitted 2022-04-29, abstract and pp. 1–3. |
| 2 | **Verified theorem.** | Guccione–Guccione–Valqui define \(B\) as the minimum gcd of total degrees over counterexamples and prove **Corollary 6.6 (pp. 34–35), \(B\ge16\)**. They further obtain \(B=16\) or \(B>20\), and \(B\ne2p\) for prime \(p\); they explicitly reject purported stronger bounds resting on a gap. “On the shape of possible counterexamples…,” [arXiv:1401.1784v3](https://arxiv.org/abs/1401.1784), 2016-05-30, abstract and §§6–7. |
| 3 | **Qualified, not safely settled as stated.** | Established results exclude generic degrees 1 (birational Keller theorem), 2 (quadratic extensions are Galois), 3 (S. Yu. Orevkov, *Math. USSR-Izv.* 29 (1987), 587–596, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1070/IM1987v029n03ABEH000984)), and 4 (A. V. Domrina, *Izv. Math.* 64 (2000), 1–33, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1070/IM2000v064n01ABEH000273)). Excluding 5 depends in the sources found on Vered Moskowicz’s **8-page v1 preprint**, whose Theorems 3.2 and 3.3 claim no prime extension degree in the cases \(xy\notin\mathbb C(P,Q)\) and \(xy\in\mathbb C(P,Q)\). [arXiv:2407.13795v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.13795), 2024-07-16. No journal/refereed version was found. Therefore “degree \(\ge6\)” is **conditional on that recent preprint**, not an unqualified established frontier. |
| 4 | **True only for the specified reduction stage.** | The four not-previously-discarded candidates listed below 125 are \((56,84),(66,99),(72,108),(80,120)\), respectively \(28,33,36,40\) times \((2,3)\); there are two configurations for \((72,108)\), one unresolved. See [arXiv:2204.14178v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.14178), pp. 1–3 and the §2 table. This supports neither a universal theorem about all classified pairs nor all possible counterexamples. |
| 5 | **Analogues exist, but “local-diffeomorphism counterexamples” is wrong/ambiguous.** | The relevant objects are **finite ramified/branched coverings over \(\mathbb C^2\)**, not polynomial Keller maps \(\mathbb C^2\to\mathbb C^2\). The three-sheet construction is Vitushkin’s ramified covering and Orevkov’s complex-analytic realization: S. Yu. Orevkov, “Rudolph diagrams and an analytic realization of the Vitushkin covering,” *Math. Notes* 60 (1996), 153–164, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF02305179); compare A. G. Vitushkin, “On the homology of a ramified covering over \(\mathbb C^2\),” *Math. Notes* 64 (1998), 726–731, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF02313030). G. V. Egorov constructs a **five-sheeted covering/topological counterexample in Vitushkin’s sense**, *Math. Notes* 71 (2002), 486–499 (Russian pp. 532–547), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1023/A:1014875630302). The accessible abstracts specify target/base \(\mathbb C^2\) and ramified/exotic-covering category, but do not identify the source as \(\mathbb C^2\); it should not be silently assumed. An unbranched connected covering of simply connected \(\mathbb C^2\) cannot supply a nontrivial 3- or 5-sheet example. |
| 6 | **Moh bound verified as the published claim; proof-detail caveat material.** | T.-T. Moh, “On the Jacobian conjecture and the configurations of roots,” *J. Reine Angew. Math.* **340** (1983), 140–212, states the characteristic-zero plane result for \(\max(\deg P,\deg Q)\le100\). Later specialists report complete detail only for some cases: Guccione et al., [arXiv:2204.14178](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.14178), pp. 1–3, say Moh fully details only the smallest case; Borisov says the terminal \((99,66)\) argument is sketchy, though later calculations rule it out: “Frameworks for Two-Dimensional Keller Maps,” *EJC* 27(3) (2020), P3.54, pp. 14–15, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.37236/9210). Nguyen’s **Theorem 3.6** gives \(\le104\) and Proposition 3.7 lists the four degree-105 residues; [arXiv:1902.05923v5](https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.05923), published *Quaest. Math.* 48(2) (2025), [DOI](https://doi.org/10.2989/16073606.2025.2482655). Guccione et al.’s Newton-polygon/equation-system classification then leaves \((72,108)\) below 125. |
| 7 | **No credible JC2 advance found in the searched window; absence result only.** | Searches through the cutoff covered the arXiv newest feed, MathOverflow, GitHub’s recently updated repositories, Crossref/OpenAlex, and general web indexes. arXiv showed no same-window JC2 item. MO questions 513387, 513390, and 513392 were announcement/work-checking or consequence questions about the new higher-dimensional claim, not validated JC2 theorems. Same-day GitHub results were verification, formalization, or exploratory repositories (including `italian-brainrot/jacobian-conjecture`, `NeilFoxAgency/jacobian-search`, and `DrAlexHarrison/jacobian-anatomy`), not public expert-validated JC2 advances. This reports search coverage, not proof that no post existed. |
| 8 | **Unsupported name.** | Borisov’s published term is **“framework”**: combinatorial data satisfying necessary Picard-group conditions; each induces a large equation system whose solution would yield a Keller map. The first framework has generic degree 16 and proposed polynomial degrees \((99,66)\), but Borisov says it probably is not realizable. No defined “minimal framework” or “minimal framework system” appears. Use “Borisov’s first framework” or “the equation system associated to a Borisov framework,” not the claimed canonical/minimal label. [Paper](https://doi.org/10.37236/9210), abstract, §§2–3, especially pp. 9 and 14–15; [arXiv:1901.04073](https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.04073). |

**Bottom line:** claims 2 and the narrowly scoped form of 4 are supported; 1 needs `<125`; 3 needs a preprint-status qualification; 5 conflates branched-cover analogues with Keller local diffeomorphisms; 6 is accurate only with the proof-detail history; 7 is a time-bounded negative search; and 8 uses no published Borisov terminology.
