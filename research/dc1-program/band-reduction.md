## 1. Setup: the tame group and the reduction invariant

An **endomorphism pair** is `(X,D)` with `[D,X]=1`; `A_1` simple ⟹ the induced
`x↦X, ∂↦D` is injective, so the whole game is **surjectivity**, i.e. whether
`⟨X,D⟩ = A_1`. The **tame group** `𝒯` is generated by:

- **affine symplectic** on `(x,∂)`;
- the **Fourier** automorphism `φ: x↦-∂, ∂↦x` (`E↦-E-1`, band-reversing);
- **pair-exchange** `S: (X,D) ↦ (D,-X)` (`[-X,D]=[D,X]=1`);
- **transvections** `exp(ad p(X)): D ↦ D - p'(X)`, `X ↦ X` (any polynomial `p`),
  and the mirror `exp(ad q(D)): X ↦ X + q'(D)`, `D↦D`.

The transvection identity is `[D - p'(X), X] = [D,X] - [p'(X),X] = 1` because
`p'(X)` commutes with `X`; and `exp(ad p(X))(D) = D + [p(X),D] + \tfrac12[p(X),
[p(X),D]] + ⋯ = D - p'(X)` because `ad(D)` acts as `d/dX` (`[D,X]=1`) and the
higher brackets vanish. **Every generator of `𝒯` preserves `[D,X]=1` and
`⟨X,D⟩`** (verifier `§1`: transvections, pair-exchange, and a Fourier witness all
keep the commutator `=1`; the transvection `D↦D-cX^2` and `X↦X+cD^2` are checked
in the crossed product). Since generation is preserved, "generates `A_1`" is a
`𝒯`-orbit invariant; minimizing over the orbit is well-posed.

**The invariant.** For a pair, `n = deg X`, `m = deg D` are the **Bernstein
degrees** (total degree in the standard `A_1` filtration `deg x = deg ∂ = 1`);
`k = max(band X, band D)` is the **band** (width of `ad(E)`-support, since
`[E, x^k f(E)] = k·x^k f(E)`). Order pairs by `(n+m, k)` lexicographically. A
pair is **reduced** if no `g ∈ 𝒯` strictly lowers `(n+m, k)`.

## 3. Dixmier's dichotomy in the E-ladder, and where the reduction stalls

**The classical input (web-verified).** Dixmier, *Sur les algèbres de Weyl*,
Bull. SMF **96** (1968), 209–242 — the leading-symbol lemma: for
`[D,X]=1` with `n+m > 2`, the top symbols Poisson-commute,
`{σ_X, σ_D} = σ_{n+m-2}([D,X]) = σ_{n+m-2}(1) = 0`, and two Poisson-commuting
**homogeneous** polynomials in two variables are, up to scalars, **powers of a
common homogeneous polynomial** `p`:

```
σ_X = α p^a,   σ_D = β p^b,   e := deg p,   n = a e,   m = b e.
```

(Verifier `§7`: `{p^3,p^2}=0` identically for a generic homogeneous quadratic
`p` by Leibniz; and, as the Dixmier lemma's converse *instance*, the
homogeneous degree-6 Poisson-centraliser of `p^3` is **exactly** `span(p^2)`,
one-dimensional — forcing the common-power shape. The general converse is the
classical lemma, not re-proved here.)

> *Literature note.* Zheglov (arXiv:2410.06959, 2024) **claims** DC1 for `A_1`;
> a Jan-2026 survey still lists Dixmier's Problem 1 as open. This program takes
> DC1 as open and uses only the 1968 leading-symbol lemma, which is uncontested.

**The reduction move.** If `b | a` then `σ_X = α(p^b)^{a/b} = α'(σ_D)^{a/b}`,
so the transvection `X ↦ X - c D^{a/b}` (an `exp(ad q(D))`) cancels `σ_X` and
**lowers `n`**; symmetrically `a | b` lowers `m` via `D ↦ D - c X^{b/a}`. A
single transvection lowers `n+m` **iff one of `a,b` divides the other**
(equivalently `m | n` or `n | m`). Concretely (verifier `§3`): the pair
`(x, ∂+x^2)` has `σ_D = x^2 = σ_X^2`, and `D ↦ D - X^2 = ∂` drops `m` from 2 to
1.

**The stall — this is the whole point.** A reduced non-generating pair with
`n+m>2` cannot admit such a move, so:

```
        reduced  ⟹  a ∤ b  and  b ∤ a  (mutually non-dividing exponents).
```

Since `a=1 ⟹ 1|b` and `b=1 ⟹ 1|a`, both exponents are `≥ 2`; the **minimum**
mutually-non-dividing pair is `(a,b) = (2,3)`. So:

> **A reduced non-generating pair sits in the coprime-power leading-form
> stratum** `σ_X = α p^a, σ_D = β p^b` with `a ∤ b, b ∤ a`, minimally `(2,3)`.

This is the **exact failure point** the classical automorphism argument
(Dixmier / Makar-Limanov degree descent) drives to zero *using the automorphism
property*. For a bare endomorphism pair that extra structure is unavailable, and
the stratum is exactly where the descent stalls. **Locating that stall is the
intrinsic definition of the walls.** (Whether a *composite* of tame moves can
still escape the stratum is precisely the open classical core — see §9, Gap 1.)

## 4. The bigraded bridge: leading forms ↔ the band, and W2 = the minimal stuck point

Map the ladder to the classical symbol via `E ↦ τ = xξ`. The leading monomial of
band `k` is `lc(a_k)·x^{k+d}ξ^{d}` at total Bernstein degree `k + 2d`,
`d = deg a_k` — **uniformly for all `k`**, negative bands included, via
`∂^j = x^{-j}(E)_j` (verifier `§2` checks the negative-band rule against the
direct `∂^j`-symbol). So the leading form of `X` is

```
σ_X = ∑_{k: k+2 deg a_k = N}  lc(a_k) · x^{k+deg a_k} ξ^{deg a_k},    N = deg X.
```

**Top-band-dominated case.** If a single band `k` reaches `N` (the generic and
minimal-degree situation), `σ_X = lc(a_k) x^{k+d}ξ^d`, a monomial; its primitive
is `p = x^{(k+d)/g}ξ^{d/g}`, `g = gcd(k,d)`, and the Dixmier exponent is
`a = gcd(k, deg a_k)`. When `a_k` has the minimal admissible degree so that the
band index itself is the exponent, `a = k`. For **D** after the `W1` gauge
`b_k=0`, the top band drops to `q = k-1`, giving `b = k-1`. Hence:

> **Top-band-dominated reduced pairs realize Dixmier exponents `(a,b)=(k,k-1)`,
> with common `p = x^2 ξ`.** These are mutually non-dividing (`gcd(k,k-1)=1`,
> `k-1 ∤ k` for `k ≥ 3`) **exactly for `k ≥ 3`**; at `k=2` we get `(2,1)`,
> `1 | 2`, which reduces — matching §2's band-2 closure.

**Verified (verifier `§2`).** The explicit W2 hatch datum
([`../band3/w2-decisive.md`](../band3/w2-decisive.md) §4) has

```
σ_X = x^6 ξ^3 = (x^2 ξ)^3,      σ_D = x^4 ξ^2 = (x^2 ξ)^2,      {σ_X,σ_D}=0,
```

i.e. **`(a,b)=(3,2)`, the global minimum `(2,3)` up to swap.** The general census
hatch (`a_k=∏_{i=0}^{k-1}(E+i(k-1))`, `b_{k-1}=∏_{j=0}^{k-2}(E+jk)`) realizes
`(k,k-1)` with the same `p=x^2ξ` for `k=3,4,5,6` (checked). The band-3 hatch is
the smallest genuinely stuck leading form — the DC1 search should read this as
*"climb from `(2,3)` upward."*

**The crucial subtlety (why leading forms cannot finish the job).** The leading
form is **blind to the wall type**: the tame consecutive cube
`a_3=E(E+1)(E+2)` and the exotic `a_3=E(E+2)(E+4)` have the **same**
`σ_X=(x^2ξ)^3` (verifier `§4`,`§5`). The shifted-power/exotic distinction is
entirely **sub-leading** — it lives one order below the symbol, exactly in the
band wall equation of §5. This is the precise sense in which *"the classical step
'leading forms cancel' fails on the wall configurations"*: at `(a,b)=(k,k-1)` no
leading-form cancellation exists, and the finer classification passes to the
necklace.

## 5. The general top wall `W(k,q)` (unconditional)

For **any** pair with `band X = k`, `band D = q` (`k ≥ q`), the highest ladder
band of `[D,X]` is `k+q ≥ 1 > 0`, so its coefficient must vanish:

```
   Q_{k+q} = b_q^{[k]} a_k - a_k^{[q]} b_q = 0        (the twisted-Wronskian wall W(k,q)).
```

No minimality, no gauge, no membership is used — it is simply the top equation of
`[D,X]=1`. On the root necklace (`δ(p)` = root divisor, `σ = T^{-1}`,
`δ(p^{[r]}) = σ^r δ(p)`) this reads, per residue coset,

```
   (σ^k - 1) · δ(b_q)  =  (σ^q - 1) · δ(a_k).
```

Because `gcd(σ^k-1, σ^q-1) = σ^d - 1` with `d = gcd(k,q)`, every solution is a
cofactor `g ∈ Z[σ]`:

```
   δ(a_k) = \frac{σ^k-1}{σ^d-1}\, g,      δ(b_q) = \frac{σ^q-1}{σ^d-1}\, g.
```

The balanced instance `q = k-1` (`d=1`) is the gatekeeper `W2q` of
[`../band3/band-k-weapons.md`](../band3/band-k-weapons.md):
`δ(a_k)=S_k g`, `δ(b_{k-1})=S_{k-1}g`, `S_n = 1+σ+⋯+σ^{n-1}`. Verifier `§4`
confirms `Q_5=0`, the necklace identity, and the cofactor factorization for W2
(`g=1-σ+σ^2`), and the general cofactor identity for `(k,q) ∈
{(3,2),(4,3),(5,4),(4,2),(6,4),(6,3)}`.

**So every pair — balanced or band-imbalanced — has its top coefficients on a
`W(k,q)` wall.** The imbalanced case (`band D` far below `band X`, e.g. after a
`b_{k-1}=0` collapse) is *not* an escape from the wall picture: it is the same
twisted-Wronskian necklace with a different `(k,q)`.

## 6. The wall dichotomy: shifted-power vs singular hatch

The cofactor `g` splits the solutions:

- **`g` effective** (all coefficients `≥ 0`): `a_k` and `b_q` are honest
  root-multiset products. The minimal `g=1` gives the **step-`d` shifted power**
  `δ(a_k)=\{0,d,\dots,k-d\}`; for coprime `(k,q)` this is the **consecutive**
  (tame) `k`-th power `a_k = c·h(E)h(E+1)\cdots h(E+k-1)`. This is the
  **shifted-power wall**.
- **`g` non-effective but `δ(a_k),δ(b_q)` effective**: `a_k` is an **exotic
  necklace** — the `S_{k-1}` (resp. `(σ^q-1)/(σ^d-1)`) factor absorbs the
  negativity. This is the **singular-hatch wall**.

The exotic branch exists **iff `(σ^q-1)/(σ^d-1) ≠ 1`, i.e. `q ∤ k`** (if `q | k`
then `d=q`, the quotient is `1`, and `δ(b_q)=g` is forced effective → only the
shifted-power branch survives). For the balanced wall `q=k-1`: `q ∤ k ⟺ k ≥ 3`.
Verifier `§5` checks: `g=1-σ+σ^2` is non-effective but its `S_k,S_{k-1}`
products are effective for **every** `k=3..7` (reproducing `band-k-weapons` W2q);
`(k,q)∈\{(4,2),(6,3),(6,2)\}` force tame; and — the punchline — the tame and
exotic band-3 tops share the leading form `(x^2ξ)^3`.

The minimal singular hatch is band 3, `g = 1-σ+σ^2`:

```
   a_3 = E(E+2)(E+4)   (roots {0,-2,-4}),     b_2 = E(E+3)   = W2.
```

Its downstream fate is the sibling corpus: `Im Φ(W2) = (E(E-1)(E+1))`, the
obstruction collapses to the single slope `R(1)=1`
([`../band3/w2-theory.md`](../band3/w2-theory.md), re-verified `§6` here:
`λ_r(E)=r+4`, `λ_{-4}(E)=0`, filler divisibility by `D`), which is **achievable**
([`../band3/w2-decisive.md`](../band3/w2-decisive.md)) yet whose **combined
slope+tail system is the unit ideal at `d ≤ 4`**
([`../band3/w2-verdict.md`](../band3/w2-verdict.md)).

## 8. The exact failure point (intrinsic wall definition)

Three independent thresholds coincide at `k = 3`, and this coincidence is the
intrinsic content:

| mechanism | reduces / closes for | first stalls at |
|---|---|---|
| Dixmier exponents `(k,k-1)` mutually non-dividing (§3–§4) | `k ≤ 2` (`1∣2`) | `k = 3` `(2,3)` |
| quantum gatekeeper `W2q` cofactor effectivity (§5–§6) | `k ≤ 2` (`S_1=1`) | `k = 3` (`g=1-σ+σ^2`) |
| band-2 tame closure (§2) | `k ≤ 2` | `k = 3` |

The classical automorphism descent "cancel the leading forms" **fails exactly**
where the exponents become mutually non-dividing, which the bigraded bridge (§4)
identifies with the band reaching `3` and the leading form becoming
`(x^2ξ)^k`; and the finer, sub-leading split into tame vs exotic is governed by
whether the necklace cofactor can be taken effective. **A "wall" is precisely a
`W(k,q)` locus at which the Dixmier reduction has stalled (`a∤b, b∤a`) and the
cofactor is forced non-effective (singular hatch) or is effective but the
telescoping descent does not propagate (shifted power).**

**Bounded / finite evidence (explicit scope):**
- Census-hatch leading forms and exponents computed for `k=3..6`; effectivity
  for `k=3..7`. General `k` is the written necklace/base-representation argument
  (`band-k-weapons`), not a per-`k` computation here.
- The Dixmier-lemma converse is verified as a single instance (`centraliser of
  p^3 = span(p^2)`), not re-proved in general; the general statement is the
  cited 1968 lemma.
- The W2 downstream fate (`R(1)=1` achievable, combined system unit at `d≤4`) is
  imported from the sibling memos (`w2-decisive`, `w2-verdict`), bounded degree.

**Open / NOT claimed — the named unhandled configurations:**

1. **Composite-move escape (= the classical DC1 core).** Reducedness is proved
   here only against **single** transvections. A *sequence* of tame moves that
   temporarily raises `n+m` before lowering it could still escape the
   `(a∤b,b∤a)` stratum. Ruling this out is exactly the endomorphism analogue of
   Dixmier's automorphism descent and is **open** — it is DC1 itself. The
   theorem is conditional on "no such composite escape," equivalently, it
   classifies the **fixed points of single-transvection reduction**, not proven
   to be all `𝒯`-minima.
2. **Shifted-power descent.** A shifted-power top (effective cofactor) has the
   *clean* leading form `p=x^2ξ` and is expected to reduce further by the
   classical telescoping tower (the positive control `X=U^k-∂/κ`, `D=κU`), but
   `W(k,q)` only constrains the top **two** bands; whether the shifted-power
   structure **propagates** to sub-leading orders and lowers the band is not
   proved — this is the difference-analogue of the Abhyankar–Moh / Newton-polygon
   step and remains open. Here shifted-power is *listed as a wall*, per the
   deliverable's option (b), not shown to reduce.
3. **Exponent formula beyond top-band-dominated.** When a single band dominates
   the Bernstein top, `p` is the monomial `x^u ξ^v` and `(a,b)=(k/w, q/w)`,
   `w=u-v` (verified for the hatch: `w=1`, `(a,b)=(k,k-1)`). If **several** bands
   reach the top, `p` is a non-monomial common form and the exponents are not
   pinned by the band indices alone; that general classification is not done.
4. **Singular-hatch realizability.** Whether any singular hatch actually carries
